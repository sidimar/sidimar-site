Know more about this project

What is?

School Garden is a program to promote healthy eating and accessibility for all through the cultivation of organic gardens in schools. The program connects universities and schools in a joint project where elementary students and schools are benefited.

How it works

Joined schools and universities are contacted by the program for allocation of interested students. College and school students make together a work plan and make the distribution of the tasks in the care of the garden. Completed the 12-month cycle, students who participated in the project become guardians of the new class, keeping the garden and multiplying knowledge and benefits.

Which benefit the university gets?

School Garden is an opportunity for graduate students perform practical activities related to their training areas, thus fulfilling the workload requirements of their courses. And the work on a project that will impact the entire community.

Which benefit the school gets?

An organic garden and healthy and fresh ingredients for school lunches! Furthermore, the school contributes to the nutritional education of their students and offers them a quality complementary activity.

Why students should participate?

Get healthy lunch, knowledge about food and care of the garden. Be health, more willing and better performance in class. There are many benefits, aren't there?
